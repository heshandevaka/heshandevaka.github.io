About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Electrical, Computer, and Systems Engineering at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, Troy, New York, advised by Prof. Tianyi Chen. My research interests lie in multi-objective optimization (MOO), with applications in multi-task learning (MTL), multi-modal learning (MML), large language model (LLM) post-training, and reinforcement learning (RL). I am passionate about designing principled optimization methods, advancing algorithmic theory, and developing efficient implementations that balance competing objectives in modern machine learning systems. My work aims to create robust, scalable solutions that address the increasingly complex trade-offs inherent in contemporary AI.
